Context

The set-up. Refined copper has decoupled from its conversion layer. LME 3M holds $13,483/t, up 4.6% over four weeks, while concentrate scarcity has compressed treatment and refining charges to multi-year lows. Chinese and Indian smelters expanded capacity through 2024-25 on the assumption that mine supply would keep pace; it has not. Grade decline at major South American operations and slower brownfield ramps have left converters bidding aggressively for feed. The result is a margin transfer from smelter to miner that the refined price alone disguises, and which term negotiations this quarter will crystallise.

The implication. Smelters carrying spot-exposed feed will see Q3 EBITDA erode sharply, while integrated miners and toll-contract holders capture the upside. Indian converters with secured concentrate term-books are best positioned; those reliant on spot face utilisation cuts. Buyers should expect refined premiums to firm as discretionary smelter output trims. We see the squeeze persisting through at least two term cycles barring a concentrate supply surprise. Position copper-exposed equities by balance-sheet feed security rather than headline production. Full positioning, term-curve scenarios and counterparty screens in Plavena Trading Prospects p.9.

Deep Dive · The Lead Story

The Copper Margin Has Left The Smelter

Refined copper looks healthy at $13,483/t. The conversion economics beneath it have collapsed. Treatment and refining charges sit near multi-year lows, transferring the entire value of tightness from converter to miner. Asia built the overcapacity; Asia now eats the squeeze. This is not a price story — it is a margin-distribution story, and it determines which balance sheets survive the term cycle.

The mechanics are unforgiving. A smelter earns its margin from the treatment and refining charge it deducts when converting concentrate to metal — not from the metal price itself. When concentrate is scarce, miners auction feed to the highest bidder and TC/RC compresses toward zero. That is precisely the regime now. Mine supply growth has lagged on grade decline at flagship South American assets and delayed brownfield ramps, while Chinese and Indian smelter capacity added through the prior cycle still demands feed. The arithmetic leaves converters with negative or near-negative processing margins on spot tonnes, even as the LME screen prints a firm and rising refined number.

Resolution comes through utilisation, not price. Marginal smelters — typically spot-exposed Chinese capacity — will trim runs or defer maintenance restarts, eventually tightening refined supply and firming premiums. That feedback loop is slow; it plays out over quarters, not weeks. In the interim, integrated producers and toll-contract holders harvest the gap. We expect at least two term cycles of pressure before capacity rationalisation rebalances the chain. The investable distinction is feed security: screen copper equities by the share of concentrate under term versus spot, not by nameplate tonnage. Headline production growth into a TC/RC trough is a margin liability, not an asset.

Feed Security Decides The Survivors

The next concentrate term negotiation is the event that matters. Benchmark settlements set the annual floor under which spot trades, and a low settlement locks distressed economics into 2027 for any smelter without captive mine supply. Indian converters fall into two camps. Those — like the better-integrated names — that secured multi-year term tonnage ahead of the squeeze will run near full and report stable conversion margin. Those reliant on the spot concentrate market, including some recently commissioned capacity, face the choice between processing at a loss to hold customers or cutting utilisation and ceding volume. Neither is attractive. For buyers of refined cathode, the lesson is to anticipate firmer premiums as discretionary output trims, and to lock physical supply from integrated sources rather than merchant smelters whose continuity is now in question.

Watch three signals into Q3: the benchmark term TC/RC settlement, Chinese smelter maintenance schedules, and any guidance on utilisation from listed Asian converters. A coordinated capacity trim would mark the bottom for treatment charges and the top for refined premiums. Absent that, the margin drain continues and the equity divergence between integrated miners and pure-play smelters widens further. Position accordingly.

Goa Restart Adds Pressure To Seaborne Iron Ore

India's iron-ore exports jumped roughly 38% month-on-month as Goan mines restart and feed the seaborne market. With 62% Fe holding at $111.65/dmt, the incremental tonnage caps any rally and reshapes the marginal-supply picture for Asian mills.

Goa's return adds an estimated 4.2 Mt of annualised supply into a seaborne market already adequately fed. The benchmark sits at $111.65/dmt, up a modest 3.9% YTD and just 2.1% over four weeks — a market grinding sideways rather than trending. Indian exporters, incentivised by domestic price spreads and a softer rupee, are clearing lower-grade material into China and Southeast Asia. That displaces marginal seaborne tonnes and pressures higher-cost producers. For Indian miners like NMDC, the volume story is positive even as price stays capped; for the global majors, Indian supply is a creeping share-loss risk into the second half. The grade mix matters — much of India's export is below 62%, so the benchmark understates the discount-tier pressure.

— India angle
